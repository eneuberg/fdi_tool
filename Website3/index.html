The Tool

This online tool provides policymakers and investment promotion agencies (IPAs) with a means of assessing and quantifying incoming FDI projects and their sustainable development contributions using a set of indicators along economic, social, governance and environmental dimensions addressing several of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, such as:

This tool introduces a range of sector-based indicators which help highlight the risks and opportunities of projects and provide the user with an overall score to assess a project. The evaluation highlights potential shortcomings of projects along the four four abovementioned dimensions to meet a country's development strategies and needs.

In addition to these sector-based indicators, the second edition of the tool, to be launched later this summer, will feature general indicators and will allow the user to change the weight of individual indictors in the overall evaluation to match the user's developmental priorities.

To find out more please consult the working paper developing the first set of indicators or enroll in ESCAP's e-learning course providing an introductory overview of the indicators.

How to use the Tool

Simply select the sector associated with the inward FDI project you are looking to evaluate.

You will then be guided through a range of questions relating to indicators in the economic, social, governance and environmental dimensions.

Some questions require a numerical value as percentage

Some questions require yes or no

After completing all questions, the user will be given three results.

A total score. If the total score is >X … if it is

A figure comparing the risks and opportunities associated with the proposed project.

A score for each dimension (economic, social, governance, environmental), highlighting which dimensions may be a stumbling bloc, i.e. lagging in their contribution to sustainable development and need further attention by the investor proposing the project.

Background

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) can contribute to the sustainable development of home and host countries through creating jobs, generating economic growth and development, helping to alleviate poverty eradication, improving access to energy, infrastructure, health and environment. While FDI can generate significant positive development results, this outcome is not always the primary objective of the private multinational enterprises (MNEs) that control the vast majority of FDI flows. These enterprises seek strategic investments that will increase their global competitiveness and profitability.

Governments should therefore prioritize attracting investment projects from MNEs that maximizes the positive and minimizes the negative developmental impacts of the investment. However, to attract and approve sustainable FDI projects, countries must have tools to evaluate the sustainability inward FDI projects and the extent to which they are aligned with the sustainable development priorities of that country.

About ESCAP’s FDI Work

The United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Asia and the Pacific (ESCAP) is providing practical technical assistance, capacity-building and policy advice to its member States. ESCAP is supporting member State IPAs on how to attract FDI for sustainable development in the short-term within their current business climate. This approach helps to ensure that countries are not only attracting more of the “right kind“ of FDI in the short term, but that they can continue to work on making the necessary changes to the overarching investment climate and policies in the medium- and long-term to boost even more FDI in sustainable development in the future. For this matter, ESCAP has been working with several of its member States to design and implement FDI attraction action plans to boost investment in the next 2-5 years in climate action, healthcare, digital transformation and gender equality. In addition, ESCAP is providing tailored capacity-building to all of its member States focused on promoting, attracting and facilitating FDI in as well as for sustainable development

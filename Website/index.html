How does it work
Background

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) fuels sustainable development by creating jobs and driving economic growth. While private multinational enterprises (MNEs) prioritize profitability, governments should focus on attracting FDI projects that maximize positive impacts. To do so effectively, countries need tools to evaluate the sustainability and alignment of inward FDI with national development priorities.

The Tool

This online tool provides policymakers and investment promotion agencies (IPAs) with a means of assessing and quantifying incoming FDI projects and their sustainable development contributions using a set of indicators along economic, social, governance and environmental dimensions addressing several of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, such as goal 3 (good health and well-being), 5 (gender equality), 7 (affordable and clean energy), 8 (decent work and economic growth), 9 (industry, innovation and infrastructure) and 13 (climate action), inter alia.

How to Use

To use the tool, simply select the sector associated with the inward FDI project you are looking to evaluate. You will then be guided through a range of questions relating to indicators in the economic, social, governance and environmental dimensions. Some questions require a numerical value as percentage (e.g., % of generated profits reinvested into local research and development) or yes or no (e.g., MSME contributes to local labour force upskilling to ensure local employment opportunities across the value chain (Y/N).
